Right now, I'm continuing on this journey by building DISCO. A platform which helps guides professionals to find their unique style and voice. Where we teach soft-skills and practical frameworks, in a no-nonsense, yet empathetic way.

Complexity Research

When we started LIS back in 2018 we outlined 4 complex challenges we knew students would have to answer in their lifetimes. They were: vaccine rollouts in a pandemic, dealing with mass misinformation, supply chain breakdowns and what to do when gene-drives get out of control. Little did we know three of those would engulf the world two years later.

The climate crisis, the rampantly rising global inequality, the fragility of all our major infrastructures. All of these and all the world's most pressing problems are "complex". They can't be broken down into neat little chunks, they can't be solved in silo's or by arbitrary disciplines. This is by LIS had to be Interdisciplinary, to bridge those gaps.

Complexity is the single most important topic we should be researching. This is why I spend whatever time I have doing fundamental research on new statistical techniques to help us quantify and navigate complex decisions. Then translating them into techniques which can be used by leaders to make real positive change.
