A brief history

Started my first business at the tender young age of 17, then was the youngest person accepted into YC, all the way back in 2008. We built a distributed hotel-chain called onefinestay (think complex logistics business, mixed with super high-end brand). Scaled it across 4 countries, with six hundred people who loved working there. Eventually sold it for a few hundred million dollars.

More recently I've helped build London Interdisciplinary School. “The most radical new university to open in decades” according to the Times. Where we prepare people for the perilously complex and uncertain future which consuming us alive (see below).

Right now, I'm building disco, a platform which guides future leaders to find their style and voice. We teach soft-skills and practical frameworks, in a no-nonsense, yet empathetic way.

Complexity Research

When we started LIS back in 2018 we could see the world's biggest problems were all complex. So we outlined 4 complex challenges we knew students would have to answer in their lifetime, they were: vaccine rollouts in a pandemic, dealing with mass misinformation, supply chain breakdowns and when gene-drives get out of control. Two years later, the pandemic hit and we all lived through three of them.

Complexity is the single most important topic we should be researching. It underpins the climate crisis, the rampantly raising global inequality, the fragility of all our major infrastructures. This is why I spend whatever left-over time I have doing fundamental research on new statistical techniques to help us quantify and navigate complex decisions.
